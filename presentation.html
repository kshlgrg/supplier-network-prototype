The problem

Most supplier decisions are made with partial visibility.

Small teams often know which supplier feels risky, but they do not have a simple way to connect dependency, reliability, lead time, disruption likelihood, and cost into one decision workflow.

Design process

Designed by removing friction before adding sophistication.

The process focused on making the first user journey extremely simple while still preserving the data needed for real network optimization.

User journey

One clear path from landing to decision.

1LandingUser starts from a simple showcase page and creates a workspace.
2Sign upOne-question-at-a-time flow collects only essential business context.
3Add infoUser enters suppliers, buyers, dependence, risk, cost, and recovery data.
4AnalyzeDashboard, map, risk, comparison, and simulation screens update from state.
5ActOptimization recommends allocation changes with reasons and constraints.

Analytics model

Risk is shown as uncertainty, not false certainty.

Monte Carlo simulator

Runs scenario draws using planning horizon, iterations, demand volatility, recovery buffer, supplier weight, disruption probability, lead time, recovery days, and annual spend.

Design principles

The interface is intentionally simple without reducing capability.

Progressive disclosure

Ask basics first, reveal deeper supplier fields only when needed.

Immediate feedback

Forms update charts, graph, checklist, recommendations, and optimizer outputs.

Recognition over recall

Plain labels and checklists make the next step visible.

Explainability

Recommendations include the reason and constraint behind each suggested share.

Recoverability

User can edit profile, reset workspace, and revise partner data.

Trust

Prototype analytics are labeled clearly and limits are documented.
