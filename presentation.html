Supplier Helper

A browser-based prototype that guides a user from first landing to supplier data entry, risk understanding, partner comparison, Monte Carlo simulation, and optimized allocation recommendations.

The problem

Most supplier decisions are made with partial visibility.

Small teams often know which supplier feels risky, but they do not have a simple way to connect dependency, reliability, lead time, disruption likelihood, and cost into one decision workflow.

The product turns supplier data into a next action: who to trust, where risk sits, and how work should be allocated.

Target user

Built for a normal operator, not only an analyst.

Owner

Business owner

Needs a simple view of supplier dependence, immediate risks, and practical actions.

Ops

Operations manager

Needs to understand lead times, recovery days, delivery reliability, and backup capacity.

Buy

Procurement lead

Needs to compare suppliers, tune tradeoffs, and explain allocation decisions clearly.

Core features

The prototype is not just screens; inputs change outputs.

Dashboard

Shows readiness, KPIs, alerts, health trend, and supplier risk distribution.

Network map

Uses D3 to show supplier and buyer relationships as a weighted graph.

Add Info

Lets the user edit profile, add partners, and change sharing preferences.

Pick Best

Compares suppliers with adjustable price, reliability, delivery, and risk weights.

Problems

Surfaces risk blocks, critical alerts, timeline, and Monte Carlo scenario outputs.

Best Plan

Generates an allocation table that balances cost, resilience, and constraints.

Analytics model

Risk is shown as uncertainty, not false certainty.

Monte Carlo simulator

Runs scenario draws using planning horizon, iterations, demand volatility, recovery buffer, supplier weight, disruption probability, lead time, recovery days, and annual spend.

Design principles

The interface is intentionally simple without reducing capability.

Progressive disclosure

Ask basics first, reveal deeper supplier fields only when needed.

Immediate feedback

Forms update charts, graph, checklist, recommendations, and optimizer outputs.

Recognition over recall

Plain labels and checklists make the next step visible.

Explainability

Recommendations include the reason and constraint behind each suggested share.

Recoverability

User can edit profile, reset workspace, and revise partner data.

Trust

Prototype analytics are labeled clearly and limits are documented.
